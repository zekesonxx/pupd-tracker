Daily Crime Log

In accordance with the 1998 Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act (Clery Act), an electronic version of the Daily Crime Log is posted on our website. Our daily crime log provides information about crimes, which have been reported within Clery geography and our patrol jurisdiction. While the daily crime log for the previous 60 days is available online, past logs are available at Purdue University Police Department. Understanding how the information is compiled is important to developing an accurate picture of crime in the greater campus community.

The incidents detailed in this log are provided according to criteria set forth in the federal Clery Act including current disposition. Not all of these crimes have been reported to the Purdue University Police Department for the purpose of police services. Rather, many of these crimes have been reported to university employees known as “Campus Security Authorities” Vice President for Ethics and Compliance (VPEC) who are obligated by federal law to bring such incidents to the attention of the university police department, so the campus community may be notified of ongoing public safety issues. Many crime victims do not want immediate police intervention and may elect not to pursue a criminal investigation.”

A printed copy of the Daily Crime Log is also available at the Purdue University Police Department, located at 205 South Martin Jischke Drive, West Lafayette, Indiana.
